About Me

Dr. Joshua Sherman Bartholomew is the Assistant Professor of Ethics, Church and Society at Saint Paul School of Theology. His academic research and forthcoming book, "Black Theology and The Black Panthers," deals with the relationship between economic justice and racial equality within the U.S. and its transnational range of influence. Before earning his Ph.D. in Religious and Theological Studies from the joint Ph.D. program at the University of Denver and Iliff School of Theology, Dr. Bartholomew first acquired his Bachelor of Science in Psychology with a minor in Theology from Fordham University's Rose Hill campus in the Bronx, NY, and then earned a Master of Divinity in Systematic Theology from Union Theological Seminary in New York City. While at Union, Dr. Bartholomew served in ministry at the historic Abyssinian Baptist Church in Harlem, NY.

Upon the completion of his Ph.D., Dr. Bartholomew accepted a position as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. His postdoctoral research project was the first systematic study of online white supremacist ideological narratives for the purpose of evaluating peer-to-peer anti-hate campaigns. Following his postdoc, and directly prior to accepting his current professorate position at Saint Paul School of Theology, Dr. Bartholomew returned to ministry and was the Youth Pastor of the historic Twelfth Baptist Church in his hometown of Boston, MA. In his capacity as Youth Pastor, Dr. Bartholomew was also a Research Coordinator for the Black Christian Experience Resource Center, formerly known as the Institute for the Study of the Black Christian Experience. Dr. Bartholomew is an experienced community organizer, DEI and Antiracism consultant, and researcher who has been able to blend his academic interests with his passion and commitment to continuing the struggle for social change.
