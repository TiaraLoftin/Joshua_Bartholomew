Research




What is the relationship between economic justice and racial equality?



Dr. Bartholomew’s primary research deals with the relationship between economic justice and racial equality. To this end, his dissertation, Economic Liberation from the Margins: The Power of Racial Politics in Social Change, was the first, original attempt to write a critical constructive relationship between Black Theology/Womanism and The Black Panther Party’s economic philosophies. By prioritizing Womanist methodologies, Dr. Bartholomew’s critiques capitalism and Eurocentric paradigms of economic justice and its relationship to the margins. There has been an absence of such a critical intellectual project. Dr. Bartholomew’s critical dialogue between Black and Womanist ethicists and economic social theorists offers a basis for moving Christian ethicists away from Eurocentric models of economic justice that eschew experiences from the margins of the colonial world. To date, there has been no such attempt in relation to Black Liberation Theology.





How do we acknowledge race without reinforcing racism?



Dr. Bartholomew has extensive experience of successfully developing empirical skills for social science research even in other areas and academic fields relevant to theological education. Dr. Bartholomew’s research as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health evaluated programs aimed at preventing violent extremism. Given that racism and violence are not only issues of (social) ethics but also of public health, Dr. Bartholomew specifically contributed to his research team qualitative data to provide the first systematic study of white supremacist ideological narratives with the purpose of analyzing people’s ability to discredit extremist propaganda.





How can we reimagine youth and young adult ministries?



As research coordinator and representative on the Equip Project, the Lilly Grant received by Gordon-Conwell (GCTS) under the leadership of Dr. Emmett Price and his team through the Institute for the Study of the Black Christian Experience (ISBCE), Dr. Bartholomew is aware of the changing concerns and priorities of young people in ministry. Dr. Bartholomew is committed to growing and adapting the church to meet emerging needs. Together with projects like #EQUIP, Dr. Bartholomew’s research provides access to spiritual and educational services to adolescents and young adults, fully integrating the cultural competence of innovative ministerial leadership.
